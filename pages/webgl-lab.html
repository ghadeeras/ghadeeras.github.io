Help

This is a simple utility page to experiment with WebGL Shaders:

  • In the left-side section, you could select from few sample shaders. Once selected, their code will appear in the text areas.
  • You could edit the code, if you are familiar with GLSL ES 2.0.
  • To make the shaders effective, press the "Compile & Link" buton. This will also refill the drop-downs for mouse position bindings.
  • In the right-side section, you could control the rendering mode (mesh vs filled surface).
  • You could also control the level of detail (LOD in 1 .. 100).
  • You could also select which uniform variable (declared in the shaders code) is bound to which mouse coordinate.
  • If you "drag" the mouse over the middle section (the square section), the bound uniform variables will change according to the mouse position, and the scene will be animated accordingly.
  • Some unifrom variables are multidimensional. For example, if unifrom variable myVariable has two dimensions, it will appear in the drop-downs as myVariable[0] and myVariable[1].
